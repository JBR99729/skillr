Year 1 Maths Focus

Learning area

Mathematics

Number, counting, place value and early number reasoning.

Year level

Year 1

Designed for early primary students building confidence with number sense.

What Students Learn

Students practise reading, writing and ordering numbers to 120, matching numerals with quantities, counting on and back, and using place value language to describe tens and ones.

  • Count collections accurately to 120.
  • Read and write numerals within 120.
  • Order numbers from smallest to largest.
  • Identify one more, one less and number patterns.
  • Use tens and ones to describe two-digit numbers.

Teaching Activities

1. Count and Collect

Ask students to collect small groups of objects and count them aloud, then record the matching numeral.

2. Number Line Jump

Use a number line to count forwards and backwards, then identify the number before and after a given value.

3. Make the Number

Students build numbers using counters, linking cubes or base-ten materials to show tens and ones.

4. Compare and Order

Compare two or more numbers and place them in order from least to greatest using number cards.

5. Numbers in Real Life

Find numbers on house signs, calendars, labels and classroom materials to show how numerals are used every day.

6. Tens and Ones Talk

Say numbers using place value language such as “4 tens and 3 ones” to strengthen number sense.

Questions to Ask Students

  • Which number is larger?
  • What number comes before or after this one?
  • How many tens and ones are in this number?
  • Can you show the same number another way?
